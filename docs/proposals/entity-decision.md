# Entity Decision: How to Sign the Work

**Status:** DECIDED — Option B (2026-04-02). Personal name for standards/research, CTE for commerce. Revisit after first paying customer.

---

## The Question

When submitting to AIUC-1, signing a consulting engagement, or putting a name on a landing page — who is the entity?

Three options. Each has tradeoffs.

---

## Option A: Michael Saleme, Independent Researcher

**What it looks like:** Personal GitHub (msaleme/), personal DOIs, personal name on AIUC-1 submission.

| Pro | Con |
|-----|-----|
| Zero setup cost — ready now | No liability shield for consulting |
| Clean for academic submissions | Cannot raise capital or take investment |
| No entity maintenance overhead | Enterprise procurement may require a registered entity |
| Research credibility is personal | IP ownership ambiguous if collaborators join |

**Best if:** The near-term priority is standards submissions and research credibility, with no consulting engagement expected in the next 90 days.

---

## Option B: CTE (Cognitive Thought Engine) as the Commercial Entity

**What it looks like:** CTE signs contracts, CTE appears on landing page, research still attributed to Michael Saleme personally.

| Pro | Con |
|-----|-----|
| Entity already exists (if registered) | Ties the security harness to CTE's broader identity |
| Can invoice, sign contracts, hold IP | If CTE has other activities, enterprise buyers may be confused |
| Can take investment | HRAO-E and the harness may have different strategic trajectories |
| Separation of research (personal) from commerce (CTE) | |

**Best if:** CTE is the intended commercial vehicle and the harness is a CTE product line.

---

## Option C: New Entity for the Security Harness

**What it looks like:** Separate LLC or similar, purpose-built for the harness business. Research attributed to Michael Saleme, commercial operations through the new entity.

| Pro | Con |
|-----|-----|
| Clean brand identity for the category | Setup cost and time (days to weeks depending on jurisdiction) |
| No brand confusion with CTE | Another entity to maintain |
| Can be named for the category (e.g., "Decision Governance Labs") | Splits attention |
| Easier to sell, spin out, or take investment specifically for this | |

**Best if:** The harness is intended to become a standalone business or acquisition target, separate from CTE.

---

## Recommendation

**Option B or C, decided this week. Not A.**

Option A works for the AIUC-1 submission (academic submissions can be personal), but it blocks the first consulting engagement and any commercial landing page. Enterprise procurement departments need an entity to send a PO to.

If CTE is already registered and operational, Option B is the fastest path — use CTE for commerce, personal name for research. If the harness is strategically distinct from CTE's other work, Option C is cleaner but slower.

**The AIUC-1 submission can go out under "Michael Saleme" regardless of entity choice** — academic/standards submissions are personal. The entity question only blocks commercial activity.
